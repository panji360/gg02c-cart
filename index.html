"Dodol" is one of the traditional snacks from Indonesia that is known to have existed since Majapahit era (14th century or even earlier)! Today, this snack can be found in Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand, where it is perhaps more well-known as "kalamea".

Face mask is always used in traditional dance performances from Java and Bali. This particular mask is based on a dance performing Panji story from Java (collection of Indonesia National Museum, Jakarta).

It is believed that this snack, which is made of fermented glutinuous rice, has existed since 14th Century or even earlier! In Java, the original setting of Panji, this snack is known as "tape ketan", but in Malaysia it is mostly known as "tapai pulut".
In the book "Hikayat Panji Semirang", which is where this story is based from, "tapai" was used in the poisoning of the Queen of Daha, Candra Kirana's mother.
